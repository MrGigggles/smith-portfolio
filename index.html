Personal Statement

My first day of grad school was also my first day teaching, when I stepped into a Music Theory 101 course at 8 AM on a Monday. I learned a hard lesson that we've all experienced at one point or another: being good at something doesn't make you good at teaching it. Two years later, I left music school as a much better teacher than that first day, but I never forgot that gap in effective learning design practice that was so apparent on that first day.

Ten years later, I've learned how to fill that gap. With seven years of experience in eLearning development and LMS administration, combined with a master's degree in instructional design. My design process is rooted in: Adult Learning Theory to create experiences that actually match way people learn; sound application of Multimedia Principles so that technology supports learning instead of getting in the way of it; and, Constructivist Learning Theory that meets people where they are and builds them up through well-scaffolded learning experiences.
